Chapter 3

Clothes greatly vary from person to person. Most people wear regular clothes while others not so regular.
I see everything from jeans to furry Eskimo snowsuits to ball gowns to the kind of mismatched clothing a homeless person or
what someone from a third world country would wear. The angels are similar in their dress code but some wear a long simple white robe,
the same kind that my angel is wearing.

I glance down at myself and notice that I’m wearing an old pair of jeans and a red T shirt. Somehow I had magically changed from my pajamas
to something I would normally wear. I frown, wondering how… Magic. That must be the answer. Or God. With him anything’s possible.

As the day light quickly fades my angel leads the way to a comely brick building. A sign in the front reads "Sixteen". My age.

As we come to a stop at the door the angel gives a few short knocks. I look over at him, a questioning look in my eyes.

He notices and says, "This is where you’ll be staying."

It is then the door opens with a motherly looking angel behind it. She has a kind expression on her face and has long curly golden blonde
hair that goes down to her waist. Despite her wise maternal expression she looks very young, in her early twenties. However, that doesn't say
much seeing as all the angels I've seen seem very young and perfect despite their apparent age.

The angel gives a soft smile and says in a voice full of sugar, honey, and sweetness and at the same time kindness, "Welcome Kaitlyn."

I stare at her and ask, "How do you know my name?"

She simply replies, "The Word of God."

The answer is left at that when she turns to my angel. "Be here at eight tomorrow."

"You're coming back?" I ask him.

"Yes. It's my duty to accompany you during your stay. We are to meet every day at least once. As for now I shall leave you in Cristina's
capable care along with the others of your age."
Cristina opens the door wider and I take a step inside but then glance back. "I guess I'll see you tomorrow..." I trail off awkwardly.

"Gabriel," he says, providing his name.

"As in the Gabriel?" I ask, eyes widening.

He gives a small wry smile and says, "No. I haven't been an angel that long."

"How long have you been an angel?" I ask, curiosity getting the better of me.

"That question will be better answered tomorrow," he replies gently. "Goodnight," and with that he turns and leaves.
