Chapter 3

The first thing I notice is that we are standing on a grassy hill. Right after that I realize we are overlooking a large city.
However, it isn't like any other city I've seen. It is surrounded by grasslands on one side (the side we are on), large forests on another side,
an ocean (I do mean an ocean, not a lake) on another, desert on yet another, and opposite that tundra. That isn't even the most surprising part.

The city itself is made up of low buildings only, no sky-scrapers or high risers. In the middle of the city there is an enormous park,
five times the size of our Forest Park. Inside the park are life-size replicas of monuments from all over the world. I could see the Eiffel Tower,
the Statue of Liberty, the Arch, and the Leaning Tower of Pisa. There were even the pyramids, the Great Wall of China, Mount Rushmore, and much more.

"Where are we?" I ask in amazement.

"Here," he replies.

"Where is here?"

"No, I mean 'Here' is the name."

"This place is named 'Here'?" I ask, perplexed.

"Yes. It is neither "there" nor "anywhere" so it has to be 'Here'."

"Isn't that confusing?" I ask.

"Only at first. You'll get used to it.

"What kind of place is Here? Is this heaven, hell, purgatory, none of the above?"

"This is simply a stop on your way. As soon as you have accepted your life as a whole you can proceed on your journey of moving on."

"How long will that take?"

"Each person is different. I've seen people stay here for as little as an hour and as long as several years."

"Years?!" I exclaim.

"Those people are usually those who die suddenly or who have a lot to think through about their lives."

It looks like I could be here for a while.

We make our way slowly down the hill and towards the city. It is almost dark by the time we finally reach it.
The angel leads the way through winding streets into the heart. The people we pass appear no different than their living counterparts,
occasionally giving us a greeting. They are of every culture, race, and age. The only exception are the very young,
those of kindergarten age and younger who are nowhere to be seen.

"Where are the young children?" I ask softly.

"Young children who haven't developed a conscience automatically accept who they are.
After they die they are simply taken to heaven," he replies just as softly.
